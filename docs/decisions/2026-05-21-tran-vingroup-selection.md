## Executive Summary

I select Vingroup JSC (VIC, HOSE) for this financial analysis. The central question is how Vingroup financed its strategic pivot into EV manufacturing via VinFast — and what that investment cycle has done to its leverage, liquidity, and cash flows over FY2024–FY2025. Three hypotheses frame the analysis: (1) debt-funded leverage has risen sharply, pressuring liquidity; (2) real estate pre-sales at Vinhomes were accelerated as an internal funding mechanism, causing unearned revenue to spike; and (3) VinFast's capex surge has outpaced operating cash generation, making Vingroup structurally dependent on external financing.

## Selection Rationale

Vingroup offers an analytically rich subject for three reasons. First, structural complexity: the group simultaneously operates a profitable real estate engine (Vinhomes) and a deep-loss EV subsidiary (VinFast), creating an intragroup subsidy dynamic that ratio analysis can isolate. Second, scale and data availability: as the largest HOSE-listed conglomerate with audited consolidated financials, HOSE filings, and Nasdaq-level VinFast disclosures, data access is comprehensive. Third, timeliness: FY2024–FY2025 captures the peak of VinFast's capital absorption phase, making it the ideal window to test whether conglomerate cash flows can sustain a high-burn subsidiary.

---

## Preliminary Observations

**H1 — Leverage has risen sharply, pressuring liquidity.**
VinFast's build-out requires massive upfront capital — plant, tooling, R&D — that cannot be self-funded in early years. I expect Vingroup to have financed this primarily through external debt (bond issuances and bank facilities), with equity growing more slowly. This implies D/E surge and a declining current ratio and interest coverage ratio by FY2025.

**H2 — Real estate pre-sales were accelerated as an internal funding mechanism.**
To generate liquidity for VinFast without additional external borrowing, I expect Vinhomes (real estate subsidiary) to have pushed pre-sales aggressively. Customer deposits collected before project completion are recorded as unearned revenue, producing a cash inflow that precedes revenue recognition. I expect a sharp increase in unearned revenue on the consolidated balance sheet and a widening gap between cash collected and revenue recognized over FY2024–FY2025.

**H3 — VinFast capex has outpaced CFO, leaving Vingroup dependent on CFF.**
I expect capital expenditure to have surged with VinFast's factory expansion, while consolidated CFO — even with real estate collections — is insufficient to cover it. Free cash flow (CFO − Capex) is expected to be deeply negative, with the gap funded by large CFF inflows from new debt or equity issuances.

## Ratio Categories Preview

Each hypothesis maps directly to the metrics tested in Stage 3:

**H1 — Leverage & liquidity**
- D/E ratio
- Net debt/EBITDA
- Interest coverage ratio (EBITD/interest expense)
- Current ratio and quick ratio

**H2 — Unearned revenue & cash conversion**
- Unearned revenue as % of total liabilities (YoY trend)
- Real estate revenue growth rate
- CFO vs. revenue recognized gap
- Cash conversion cycle

**H3 — Capex, FCF & financing dependency**
- Capex (absolute and YoY)
- Free cash flow (CFO − Capex) 
- CFO/net income ratio (earnings quality)
- CFF composition (new borrowings vs. equity issuances vs. repayments)
